# Pitch Email: Podcasts

**Target**: Local business podcasts, entrepreneur podcasts, hyperlocal/community podcasts

---

## Subject Line Options

1. "Podcast guest: Building a hyperlocal platform for my hometown"
2. "Story idea: One developer's mission to save the high street"
3. "Guest pitch: whitstable.shop founder"

---

## Email Template

**To**: [Podcast contact]
**Subject**: Podcast guest: Building a hyperlocal platform for my hometown

---

Hi [Host name],

I've been listening to [Podcast name] and really enjoyed your episode on [specific episode]. Your question about [specific topic] got me thinking.

I'm reaching out because I have a story that might resonate with your audience:

**The Story**: I built whitstable.shop, a free platform dedicated to my hometown's independent high street. It's a mobile guide to 80+ local shops, restaurants, and cafes—with reviews, maps, events, and local info. No chains, no ads, just the businesses that make Whitstable special.

**Why It Might Work for Your Show**:

Your show often covers [theme from their show]. My story touches on:

- **Building for local communities**: How hyperlocal can compete with global platforms
- **Solo founder journey**: Building a product while [working/other commitments]
- **Supporting independent businesses**: The "shop local" movement in practice
- **Privacy-first tech**: Building without invasive tracking

**Potential Topics**:

- Why hyperlocal platforms have an opportunity right now
- How small teams can compete with Big Tech on local
- The future of high streets and independent retail
- Building in public and community feedback
- Sustainable business models for local platforms

**About Me**:

I'm [Your name], a [brief background]. I built whitstable.shop because I love Whitstable's independent shops and wanted to help visitors discover them. The platform launched [recently] and has [brief traction note if available].

I'm comfortable on podcasts and can speak to both technical and business aspects. Happy to do audio or video, and I can be flexible on timing.

Would this be a fit for [Podcast name]?

Thanks for considering,

[Your name]
[Your email]
whitstable.shop

## Talking Points Preparation

Be ready to discuss:

1. **Origin story**: Why you built it, personal connection
2. **Technical decisions**: Stack choices, build process
3. **Business model**: How it's sustainable
4. **Challenges**: What was hard, what you learned
5. **Results**: Traction, shop owner reactions
6. **Future**: Where it's going, expansion plans
7. **Advice**: For others building local products

---

## Notes

- **Listen to episodes first**: Reference specific content
- **Match their format**: Some want stories, some want tactics
- **Be patient**: Podcast schedules are planned months ahead
- **Offer flexibility**: Available slots, remote-friendly
- **Prepare audio setup**: Good mic, quiet room
